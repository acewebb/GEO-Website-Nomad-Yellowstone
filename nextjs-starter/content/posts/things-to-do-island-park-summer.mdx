## Island Park, Idaho: Summer Recreation Guide

Island Park is Yellowstone's quieter gateway. Located 20 minutes from West Yellowstone, this small mountain town is the basecamp for Nomad Yellowstone ATV expeditions and a recreation paradise in its own right. Summer offers unlimited outdoor activities in a genuinely quiet setting.

**ATV Riding & Backcountry Expeditions**

Island Park is the center of Greater Yellowstone ATV culture. Nomad Yellowstone operates daily guided ATV tours April 15–October 31, offering:

- **Morning expeditions** – High-elevation ridge rides, 5–7 hours.
- **Mid-day tours** – Moderate rides, 4–5 hours.
- **Evening expeditions** – Sunset rides with wildlife focus, 3 hours.
- **Private tours** – Custom routes, groups, and skill levels.

All tours are beginner-friendly. No experience required.

**Fly Fishing Excellence**

Island Park sits at the confluence of some of North America's premier fly fishing waters.

- **Henry's Fork of the Snake River** – World-famous cutthroat and brown trout, technical fly fishing, premium guides available.
- **Pond's Lodge waters** – Accessible private water with excellent trout populations.
- **Upper Snake River** – Scenic floats with consistent fishing.

Book fly fishing guides through local shops or lodges. Early morning and evening hatches are stellar.

**Hiking & Trail Access**

Island Park has excellent trail access to National Forest lands.

- **Big Springs trail** (3 miles round trip) – Crystal-clear spring, boardwalk, scenic setting. Easiest trail in the area.
- **Mesa Falls Scenic Byway** (driving + short walks) – Overlooks Lower Mesa Falls, photography stops, interpretive signs.
- **Higher elevation trails** – Rim trails with panoramic views, accessed via Island Park roads.

**Big Springs**

A must-visit attraction. Big Springs is one of the largest freshwater springs in the US, discharging 120+ million gallons daily. The water is crystal clear, 52°F year-round.

- **Scenic boardwalk loop** – 1-mile easy walk.
- **Wildlife viewing** – Osprey, elk, waterfowl.
- **Photography** – Stunning clear-water landscapes.
- **Fishing** – Catch-and-release fly fishing in the spring outflow.

**Mesa Falls Scenic Byway**

This 28-mile scenic drive offers:

- **Lower Mesa Falls overlook** – 65-foot waterfall, interpretive signs.
- **Upper Mesa Falls** – Accessed via separate trailhead, more remote setting.
- **Wildlife habitat** – Prime bear, elk, and wolf terrain (view from vehicle).
- **Photography** – Excellent light and landscape variety.

Best driven in early morning or late afternoon.

**Local Restaurants & Services**

Island Park has minimal infrastructure compared to West Yellowstone, but essential services exist:

- **Island Park Village Resort** – Lodging, restaurant, ATV rentals.
- **Island Park Restaurant** – Local dining.
- **Gas stations** – Limited hours, fill up before expeditions.
- **General stores** – Limited supplies; stock up in West Yellowstone.

**Summer Weather**

Island Park sits at 6,000+ feet elevation. Summer temperatures average 65–75°F during the day, 45–55°F at night. Morning frost is common early summer. Afternoon thunderstorms are frequent but brief.

**Wildlife Viewing**

Island Park is prime Greater Yellowstone wildlife habitat. Morning/evening rides with Nomad Yellowstone offer excellent viewing:

- **Elk herds** – Visible throughout summer.
- **Grizzlies** – Occasionally visible, especially near streams.
- **Wolves** – Heard and seen, especially May–June and September.
- **Waterfowl** – Big Springs and Henrys Fork are prime bird habitat.

**The Island Park Strategy**

Nomad Yellowstone is based in Island Park for good reason: it's the quiet recreation epicenter of the Yellowstone region. Combine ATV expeditions with fly fishing, hiking, and Big Springs visits. Stay 3–5 days. Return to West Yellowstone for dining variety and nightlife.
