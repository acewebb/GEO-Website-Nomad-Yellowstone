Yellowstone has five entrances, but the two most popular are North (Gardiner) and West (West Yellowstone). Both are excellent, but they offer different experiences and access different park features. Your choice depends on what you want to see and how you want to travel.

## North Entrance (Gardiner, Montana)

**Key Attractions Nearby**
Mammoth Hot Springs (the park's most extensive thermal complex), Lamar Valley ("the Serengeti of North America," the best wildlife viewing), Norris Geyser Basin, and the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone.

**Special Feature: Year-Round Access**
North entrance is the only year-round entrance to Yellowstone. If you're visiting November-March, you enter here (or Northeast/East, which also stay open). Winter access is a huge advantage if you're planning a cold-season trip.

**Lamar Valley Wildlife**
Lamar Valley is legendary for wildlife—elk, bison, wolves, grizzlies, bighorn sheep. Early morning and evening in Lamar almost guarantees animal sightings. If wildlife is your primary goal, North entrance is closer to the best spot.

**Mammoth Hot Springs**
The largest thermal complex in the park. Otherworldly colors, unique geology, and accessible boardwalks. It's a major draw.

**Town Infrastructure**
Gardiner is small but functional. It has lodging, restaurants, and fuel. It's a real town (not just a tourist strip).

**Entering the Park**
You cross the Roosevelt Arch and enter immediately. It's straightforward and feels ceremonial.

**Route Limitations**
From North entrance, reaching Old Faithful and the geysers of the west requires looping south and around. It's doable but means more driving to access the entire park.

## West Entrance (West Yellowstone, Montana)

**Key Attractions Nearby**
Old Faithful (the iconic geyser), Fountain Paint Pot, Madison, and the entire western geyser basin. These are some of the park's most famous features.

**Town Infrastructure**
West Yellowstone is a full-fledged tourist town. More restaurants, more lodging options, more breweries, and more "stuff" than Gardiner. If you want comfort and services, it's better stocked.

**Geyser Access**
The west side of the park is geyser-rich. Old Faithful, Fountain, Norris—all accessible from West entrance. If geysers are your focus, West wins.

**ATV Expeditions**
Nomad Yellowstone operates from Island Park, Idaho (20 minutes from West Yellowstone). If backcountry ATV access is part of your trip, West entrance is where you base.

**Balanced Park Access**
From West, you can reach the north loop (Mammoth, Lamar) or the east/south (Grant, Yellowstone Lake). It's more central for full-park itineraries.

**Midway Geyser Basin**
Grand Prismatic Spring (the largest hot spring in the US) is closer to West entrance. It's visually stunning and worth seeing.

## What Different Travelers Should Choose

**First-Time Yellowstone Visitors with 3-4 Days**
**West entrance wins.** Proximity to Old Faithful, better town infrastructure, more restaurant/lodging variety, and good access to most major park features. You get the iconic Yellowstone experience without town infrastructure stress.

**Wildlife Enthusiasts**
**North entrance.** Lamar Valley is unmatched for animal sightings. Your best chances of seeing wolves and grizzlies are here. Early morning and evening in Lamar are magical.

**Winter Travel (Nov-April)**
**North entrance (only option).** West is closed. If you're coming in cold months, North is the only choice.

**Want ATV Backcountry Tours**
**West entrance.** Nomad Yellowstone operates from Island Park, 20 minutes from West Yellowstone. If guided ATV expeditions are part of your trip, base yourself here.

**Extended Trip (5+ Days)**
**Enter from West, stay through both.** You can enter West, do geyser basins, loop north to Mammoth and Lamar, and exit North (or vice versa). Takes more time but maximizes park experience.

**Secondary Visits (Return Trips)**
**North entrance.** Many people see the geysers first (West). If you're returning, focus on North and wildlife.

## The Real Answer

For most first-time visitors to Yellowstone: **West entrance is stronger.** Better town infrastructure, more restaurant/lodging options, easier access to iconic Old Faithful and geysers, and proximity to ATV tours if you want backcountry experience.

For wildlife-focused trips and winter visits: **North entrance is better.**

Ideally, if you have time (5+ days), use both. Enter West, experience the geysers, loop north, and explore Lamar Valley. You get the full Yellowstone experience.

Choose based on your primary interest and trip length. Both gates deliver.
