Nomad Yellowstone offers two ATV tour options: private buyouts (your group only) and group tours (you join other riders). Each structure offers different advantages. Your choice depends on your group size, budget, and what kind of experience matters most to you.

## Private ATV Buyouts

**What You Get**
Your group (6-20+ people ideally) has a dedicated guide and customizable route. You set the pace, choose stops, take breaks when you want, and design the experience around your group's preferences.

**Customization**
Want to spend an hour photographing a valley? Done. Want to hit harder terrain? Guide adjusts. Want to visit a specific area? Your route is designed around it. This flexibility is the core advantage.

**Group Dynamics**
Everyone is "yours"—friends, family, corporate team, wedding party. You control the vibe. No strangers. No compromises with unfamiliar riders.

**Cost**
$699 for the tour, split among your group. With 6 people, it's ~$116/person. With 10, it's ~$70/person. Large groups get excellent per-person value.

**Pacing**
The guide matches your group's skill level and fitness. If someone's struggling, the guide adjusts. No pressure to keep up with experienced riders.

**Photography and Stops**
You can stop as long as you want at scenic spots. A photographer can take 20 minutes framing a shot; a private tour accommodates that.

**Best For**
- Corporate groups and team building
- Large families
- Wedding parties (bachelor/bachelorette groups)
- Friends who want a full group experience
- Groups with specific route preferences
- Special occasions requiring customization

**Downsides**
Minimum group size (usually 6). Booking commitment. You're responsible for assembling your group.

## Group ATV Tours

**What You Get**
You join 4-8 other riders with one guide. Everyone is new to each other. You experience the tour together but aren't a unified team beforehand.

**Cost**
~$179-200 per seat (pricing varies). Excellent value for individuals, couples, small groups.

**Social Advantage**
You meet other travelers. People often form friendships during shared adventures. If you're traveling solo or as a couple, it's a built-in social element.

**Pace and Dynamics**
Guide balances the entire group's abilities and interests. You're riding with people of varying experience, so the pace is moderate and inclusive.

**Group Size Flexibility**
No minimum requirement. Solo travelers can book one seat. Couples take two seats. Groups of 5 can join without buying the whole tour.

**Logistics Simplicity**
Book it online. Show up. Ride. No group coordination required.

**Route Consistency**
The guide runs a proven route suitable for mixed-skill groups. Less customization but proven to be excellent.

**Best For**
- Solo travelers wanting to meet people
- Couples
- Small groups (2-4 people)
- First-time ATV riders (mixed-ability guidance is standard)
- Budget-conscious travelers
- People who prefer simplicity (book, show up, done)

**Downsides**
You're accommodating other riders' needs and pace. Less customization. You might not see every location you hoped for because the group's collective interests guide the route.

## Decision Framework

**Choose Private Buyout If:**
- You have 6+ people who want to experience the tour together
- You want a specific route or customization
- Budget per person is okay (or you're splitting costs)
- The shared experience and group bonding matter
- You have a special occasion driving the trip

**Choose Group Tour If:**
- You're solo or a couple
- You want to meet other travelers
- Budget is the primary concern
- You want simplicity (book online, show up)
- You're new to ATVing and want inclusive pacing
- You have a small group (2-4 people) and adding unrelated riders is fine

## The Real Take

For most people visiting Yellowstone as couples or individuals: **Group tour** is the right choice. Cost is better, logistics are simple, and you'll have good company.

For families, corporate retreats, bachelor/bachelorette parties, and large friend groups: **Private buyout** is worth it. The customization and group cohesion justify the logistics.

Both deliver exceptional backcountry experience. You're choosing the structure that fits your situation best.
